This is the review snapshot for PRs #56–#60. It separates retained performance
measurements on the project's main RTX 3090 from quality confirmation and
diagnostic same-device throughput on the secondary RTX 3090. Generated JSONL
and `/tmp` artifacts are intentionally not checked in; this tracked report

## Headline

- With default-off batching left disabled, the combined hot-path changes
  deliver **1.027x–1.171x paired optimizer-step speedup**, depending on matrix
  scale and NS schedule: a **2.6%–14.5% latency reduction**, not a universal
  end-to-end claim. Enabling opt-in batched NS raises the measured range to
  **1.031x–1.182x** (**3.0%–15.4% latency reduction**).
- On the large 385.9M-parameter optimizer workload, the retained default totals
  are **1.044x for NS3** and **1.027x for NS5**; opt-in batching raises them to
  **1.046x** and **1.031x**, respectively.
- The schedules tested use **three or five Newton–Schulz iterations**, not
  10–20 Muon/NS iterations. NS iteration count is distinct from the number of
  optimizer updates in a training run.
- On Qwen3-0.6B, tuned NS3 is **1.188x** as fast as classic NS5 at `3e-5`
  (3,971 vs 3,344 tok/s), with final validation loss 1.57594 vs 1.57286.
- At 2,000 Qwen updates, NS3 + NorMuon reaches 1.543885 final / 1.550765
  tail loss, effectively tied here with classic and stock NS5,
  while delivering **1.165x** classic throughput (3,889 vs 3,339 tok/s).
- At 134M scale, tuned NS3 completed 1,024 updates / 134.2M tokens with a
  strictly decreasing curve, 0.86240 final loss, and 25.2k tok/s.
- The matched 134M recommended recipe improves that endpoint to 0.84586; its
  diagnostic throughput was near-identical in the same-secondary sequence.
- Classic NS5 remains the 33M quality leader. Tuned NS3's three-seed mean final
  loss at `3e-3` is 1.19513 vs 1.16203 for classic NS5.
- Batched NS has no consistent quality penalty across three 33M seeds. Its
  retained incremental optimizer-only gain is workload-dependent:
  **1.001x–1.027x**.
- The low-memory hybrid cells use about 0.561 GiB of Qwen optimizer state
  (literal and split-LR-only are the byte-level minima), versus 0.994 GiB
  classic and 2.220 GiB AdamW, but trail the completed SFT loss leaders.

## Metric definitions

For a same-round pair with old latency `L_old` and new latency `L_new`:

```text
paired speedup       = L_old / L_new
latency reduction    = 1 - (L_new / L_old)
```

Thus, 1.182x speedup is an 18.2% rate increase but a 15.4% latency
reduction. The paired table uses the median of four same-round ratios, while
the displayed latency reduction is calculated from the displayed latency
medians; it therefore need not equal `1 - 1 / paired_speedup` exactly. In the
33M sections, AUC is the normalized trapezoidal mean across step 50–500
validation points, excluding step 0. The formal 134M AUC uses steps 128–1,024,
and the 2,000-update SFT AUC uses steps 250–2,000; both exclude step 0. Lower
is better.

Recipe shorthand: **classic NS5** is GefenMuon standard NS5, NorMuon off, with
AdamW auxiliary; **tuned NS3** changes only to tuned3; **NorMuon** turns
normalization on; **recommended** uses tuned3 + NorMuon with a Gefen auxiliary
at 0.5x LR and 1D period-one. **Stock Muon** is `torch.optim.Muon` NS5 with
AdamW auxiliary.

## Caveats

- The optimizer microbenchmark uses synthetic fixed gradients and measures only optimizer latency. Qwen device-pipeline tok/s is the model-training corroboration.
- The Qwen LR screen is one seed and 800 updates. The longer 2,000-update
  frontier is still one seed; very small loss differences need a multi-seed
  confirmation.
- The 33M model has a byte vocabulary, making its embedding/head and auxiliary
  optimizer state unusually cheap. Use Qwen for realistic vocabulary memory.
- CUDA deterministic algorithms were not forced. Seeds, immutable revisions,
  data hashes, and order hashes were recorded, but bitwise replay is not claimed.
- AdamW moments follow the BF16 parameter dtype. This is not an FP32-master,
  FP32-moment mixed-precision AdamW reference.
- The staged performance ratios depend on the tested shapes. Batching helps
  repeated eligible shapes and is not expected to scale equally everywhere.
- The raw local JSONL outputs under `benchmarks/training_matrix/out/` and the
  `/tmp/gefen-*` benchmark files are generated/ignored artifacts;
  they are not part of this PR.
